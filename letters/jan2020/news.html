New App

Segment tractogram in to fiber categories

Do you have tractography that you'd like to do a quick quality check on? Perhaps you want to see if there are biologically implausible streamlines in your whole brain tractogram or if there‚Äôs a segmentation failing to find a tract of interest. This app categorizes each streamline in your tractography model according to the regions it connects (i.e. fronto-parietal), giving you a window into your data. A quick look at the relative proportions of the output categories can help you determine if your tractography has sufficient quality and if your tract of interest is likely to be found.

See the App

fMRIPrep

This app runs fMRIPrep on the brainlife.io interface. Its pipelines process T1w, T2w, fMRI, and fieldmaps by calling a series of functions from FSL, FreeSurfer, ANTs, and nipy. It applies these tools in a principled way designed to handle common imaging artifacts and biases, and it outputs processed anatomical and functional images for further analysis. // why do you need to preprocess to begin with -- imaging who‚Äôs going to be reading this -- those data needs to be cleaned and realined even though FRMI is the best mode of processing mri data -- segue into why they need preprcoessing and why to do it on brainlife and then how it works
